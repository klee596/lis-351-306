Identity, Algorithms, and Micro-Trends

Fashion moved from about being “in” and became about defining your aesthetic. Suddenly, subcultures and micro-trends were everywhere. The question was no longer “are you in?” but “what’s your aesthetic?”

Social media drove this shift. Especially with the rise of short-form content. Platforms like TikTok, and, later, Instagram through scrollable Reels, transformed how fashion trends were created and consumed. Instead of searching through magazines, blogs, and mood-boarding, users had a constant stream of videos curated by algorithms. This led to trends growing exponentially, with new trends appearing and disappearing in a matter of weeks.

What's trending now?

End of the Clean Girl

In the early 2020s, the "clean girl" aesthetic took over the internet, with sleek hair and a minimal lifestyle. It promoted not just a fashion style but also a lifestyle. It was no longer about looking like a "clean girl" but being one.

Internet-Born Style

In the 2010s, platforms like Tumblr and early social media led fashion trends through mood boards, aesthetic communities, and viral posts. Celebrity culture set the trends for what people wore.
