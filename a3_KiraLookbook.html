What's trending now?

When 2020 hit, fashion stopped being about being “in” and became about defining your aesthetic. Suddenly, subcultures and micro-trends were everywhere. The question was no longer “are you in?” but “what’s your aesthetic?”

Social media drove this shift, especially with the rise of short-form content. Platforms like TikTok and Instagram transformed how fashion trends were created and consumed. Instead of searching through magazines or blogs, users had a constant stream of videos curated by algorithms, making trends grow exponentially.

End of the Clean Girl

In the early 2020s, the "clean girl" aesthetic took over the internet, with sleek hair and a minimal lifestyle. It promoted not just a fashion style but also a lifestyle. It was no longer about looking like a "clean girl" but being one. Alongside this, fashion shifted toward hyper-feminine looks that reinforced traditional gender roles. Influences like Nora Smith and shows such as The Secret Lives of Mormon Wives gained popularity, turning fashion into a powerful form of cultural messaging and social commentary.

Internet-Born Style

In the 2010s, platforms like Tumblr and early social media led fashion trends through mood boards, aesthetic communities, and viral posts. Celebrity culture, from Zooey Deschanel to Rihanna to early Instagram influencers, set the trends for what people wore. Together, social media and celebrity influence made fashion more personal, expressive, and connected to online communities.
